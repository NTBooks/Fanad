# Notebooks: a separate room for the big thing

Sometimes one list isn't the problem — one *life* is. The house renovation has forty tasks, and they're drowning out "call the pharmacy." Or you want work stuff somewhere your Sunday-morning `whatdo` will never surface it. Or you're organizing something bounded — a wedding, a move, an estate — that deserves its own clean pad and a door you can close.

Notebooks are exactly that: isolated sub-spaces with their own tasks, notes, and lists. Like a fresh account you can walk into and back out of.

## Turn it on, step in

```
you:    optin notebook
you:    notebook renovation
fanad:  ✓ Made a new notebook 📓 renovation and switched you in — a
        clean space for tasks, notes & lists. "notebook main" takes
        you home.
```

A new name creates the notebook on the spot. Once inside, everything works exactly as normal — capture, steps, `whatdo`, lists, notes — but it all lands *here*. Your main list neither sees nor is seen.

```
you:    get three quotes for the bathroom
fanad:  ✓ Filed: "get three quotes for the bathroom" · Home · medium
you:    whatdo
fanad:  💡 How about "get three quotes for the bathroom"?
```

That `whatdo` will only ever suggest renovation things. And back in main, the renovation's forty tasks stop shouting over your groceries.

## Where am I?

The one habit notebooks ask of you: know which room you're standing in. If you're ever unsure:

```
you:    notebook
fanad:  📓 Notebooks — a separate, private space for tasks, notes & lists.

        • work
        • renovation — you're here

        Switch or create: "notebook <name>" · back home: "notebook main"
```

`notebook main` (or just `home`) takes you back. Notebooks never nest, and switching is instant — it's normal to pop into `work` for one capture and pop back out.

## What carries across, what doesn't

- **Separate per notebook:** tasks, notes, lists — the content.
- **Shared across all your spaces:** your module choices, your mood, and your chat itself. Reminders still find you in the same conversation regardless of where they were set.
- **Private:** a notebook is yours alone. Nobody else — vouched-in friends included — can reach into it.

## Good notebook shapes

- **A bounded project:** the renovation, the move, the conference you're running. When it ends, the clutter ends with it — your main pad never absorbed any of it.
- **A role:** `work`, kept strictly apart so weekend suggestions stay weekend-shaped.
- **A person you're helping:** managing a parent's appointments and paperwork in its own space keeps their affairs coherent and out of your personal list.
- **A someday-shelf:** a `someday` notebook for the ideas you refuse to delete but don't want ranked against real life.

The test for "does this deserve a notebook?" is simple: do you want these tasks *suggested to you* alongside everything else? If yes, a category is enough. If the honest answer is "only when I've deliberately sat down for it" — that's a notebook.
